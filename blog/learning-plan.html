Learning Plan

Long term goals:

My long term career goal is to work in the tech industry. I am hoping that upon becoming a web developer, I am able to expand my skill set and work in other areas such as app development, testing or becoming a scrum master.

My long term personal goal is to use my tech skills that I get from Dev Academy to help the organisation sector.

Helps and hinders for my learning:

Attributes which will help and hinder my learning are:

My adaptability:
My adaptability helps my learning as I am open to new situations and working with people. As I am learning this new and hard skill, I am willing to put in the effort to get the most out of it.

My organisation:
Given the workload within this course, my ability to remain organised will ensure that I meet daily and weekly tasks. Not just within DA, but also outside in my personal life, it ensures that I stay committed to my other commitments.

My curiosity:
Although my curiosity has led me to my current life path, I can get too wrapped up in the finer details. I forget the bigger picture or get too caught up in details that actually do not matter. I end up wasting energy and effort.

My perfectionism:
I can spend too much unnecessary time trying to make things exactly how I envisioned it. I am learning to be okay when things are not 100% how I wanted them.

How I will execute being a student at Dev Academy:

To start the day, I always set a work goal/intention. This keeps me accountable and ensures that I am finishing tasks on time. I’ve been using my google calendar for a hearty 9 years now. It’s the only system I use to organise everything.

In terms with others, I approach all people with manaakitanga. I have many ways I emotionally regulate - I journal, meditate (not all the time), exercise and keep the social calendar booked. These activities protect my well-being and ensure that my best self can show up to learn.


Practising mindfulness is important to me so if I find that things are getting to much, I will:

1. Take a step back and reflect on what is stressing me out through journaling.
2. Consult someone in my personal support system so that I am heard.
3. Consult people in my network who have already been through DA to see if I am able to get a solution to how I am feeling.
4. Execute the solution and approach facilitators if needed.

I will start to seek help:
- When I do not understand how to fix a problem after spending my own time on it.
- If I start to notice that I am unable to enjoy things outside of DA because I am too stressed or worried about the work.

During my time at DA, I want to work on my ability to give and receive feedback, dealing with conflict/ mediating tough conversations.

I expect the facilitation team to be patient, kind and enthusiastic. I expect the facilitation team to provide me with support (even when I might not know I need it) and to provide a safe space for making mistakes and creating relationships.
